# Penyzen GTM Playbook — College-Launch Vertical

The go-to-market and channel playbook for the ratified Week-1 vertical.
Companion to [`business-strategy.md`](./business-strategy.md) (§5 has the
pressure-test that selected this vertical).

Created: 2026-05-16. Working document.

---

## 0. The one-paragraph thesis

Every spring, millions of US high-school seniors are accepted to college
and then a meaningful share never enroll because the first bills (tuition
deposit, dorm, laptop, books) hit before aid does — a measurable,
named higher-ed problem called **"summer melt"** (commonly cited at
~10–40% of low-income accepted students). Penyzen lets a student's whole
community fund those first costs, paid **directly to the institution**,
so donors know it can't be diverted and the student actually matriculates.
The channel is the people who already own the summer-melt problem: high
school counselors and college-access nonprofits.

This reframes Penyzen from "another fundraiser" to **"the platform that
gets accepted kids to actually show up to college"** — an outcome metric
the channel and investors both care about.

---

## 1. ICP (who exactly)

**Beneficiary:** a graduating senior with a college acceptance and a
funding gap — strongest with first-generation and low/moderate-income
students (the summer-melt-prone segment). Not "needs a full ride" — needs
the *gap* closed ($500–$5,000 typically: deposit, housing, laptop, fees).

**Organizer:** usually *not* the student. A parent, relative, counselor,
mentor, coach, or community sponsor who sets up and shares the campaign.

**Donor community:** extended family, family friends, church/temple/
mosque, neighbors, local small businesses, the student's teachers and
coaches, and "pay-it-forward" alumni. Aspirational, joyful giving — high
shareability.

**Channel partner (the actual customer to win):**
1. **High-school counselors / college & career centers** — one counselor
   has 30–400 seniors. The wedge.
2. **College-access nonprofits** — GEAR UP, AVID, College Possible,
   uAspire, Bottom Line, Let's Get Ready, local equivalents. They own the
   summer-melt KPI and have student rosters + trust.
3. **Faith communities & service clubs** (Rotary, Kiwanis) — recurring
   sponsors of "send a kid to college" drives.
4. **Scholarship foundations** — already cut checks to schools; Penyzen
   is the community-funded layer on top.

## 2. Positioning & messaging

Anchor everything on **direct-to-institution trust + the matriculation
outcome**, never on fees or "easy fundraising."

| Audience | Message |
|---|---|
| Donors | "Your gift pays her tuition directly to the school — not a bank account. You'll see exactly where it went." |
| Students/families | "Let your community help you get to campus. No awkward cash asks — everything goes straight to the school." |
| Counselors / nonprofits | "Close the summer-melt gap. Verified, paid directly to the institution, with a reporting dashboard for your cohort." |
| Press / VC | "The crowdfunding platform that measurably increases college matriculation — funds can't be diverted by design." |

Proof points to build toward: % of campaigns where the student
matriculated, $ delivered directly to institutions, fraud rate ≈ 0
(structural, because money never touches a personal account).

## 3. The channel motion (how you actually grow)

Penyzen does **not** acquire students one at a time. It acquires
*counselors and programs*, each of whom brings a cohort.

**Land:** one motivated counselor or one college-access program site.
**Expand:** that site's full senior cohort → the district → the program's
other sites → state counselor associations (state ASCA chapters,
conferences) → national.

Counselor enablement kit (build this for the pilot):
- A 1-page explainer + a student/family flyer (QR to a co-branded page).
- A "cohort dashboard" so the counselor sees their students' campaigns,
  $ raised, and matriculation status in one view (this is the retention
  hook — it's a tool they'll reuse every year).
- A 20-minute onboarding call script.
- Optional co-branding ("[School] College Launch, powered by Penyzen").

Channel economics: keep it free for schools/counselors/students.
Monetize via optional donor tip + payment processing (same model the
incumbents proved) and, later, B2B reporting for districts/programs.
**Do not charge the channel** — that kills the motion.

## 4. Seasonal calendar

| Window | What's happening | Penyzen action |
|---|---|---|
| **May–Jun** | Acceptances in, deposits due, grad season — peak emotion & sharing | **Peak campaign creation.** Pilot launches here. Grad-announcement sharing = built-in K-factor |
| **Jul–Aug** | Summer melt window — bills hit before aid; students fall away | **Peak urgency & conversion.** The "did they actually enroll?" outcome lands |
| **Sep–Oct** | Matriculation confirmed; first-semester surprises | Outcome reporting; testimonials; case studies for the deck |
| **Nov–Feb** | Off-season for new launches | Channel build (counselor associations, conferences), product, and **persistence/emergency-fund** campaigns (year-round) |
| **Mar–Apr** | Decision season ramps | Pre-load next cohort; counselor re-onboarding |

**Seasonality is the known weakness.** Mitigations (state these openly):
1. Expand to **college-persistence / emergency retention funds** —
   year-round, large unmet need (a student one $600 emergency away from
   dropping out). Same direct-to-institution rails.
2. Add **trade/vocational and community-college launch** — different,
   slightly staggered calendars.
3. Use the off-season for channel and product, not idle burn.

## 5. The pilot — "20 real campaigns in 30 days"

Concrete, concierge, no new code beyond the MLW (see
[`roadmap-mlw-implementation.md`](./roadmap-mlw-implementation.md)).

**Week 0 (prep):** finish the MLW; pick ONE metro; build the counselor
kit; line up 1–3 channel contacts (a counselor, a college-access program
site lead, a church youth/education director).

**Week 1:** onboard the first channel partner. Concierge-create 5–8
campaigns *with* them (you do the setup, they bring the families). Verify
the institution payee for each (this is the trust proof — get real bursar
payment instructions for ≥2 schools).

**Week 2–3:** the channel partner shares to their cohort + you support
family sharing. Target 20+ live campaigns. Instrument the 6 funnel events
(business-strategy.md §1) from day one — vertical KPIs below.

**Week 4:** first direct-to-institution disbursements executed. Capture:
$ delivered to schools, share funnel, and at least 3 family testimonials
+ 1 counselor testimonial.

**Success bar for the pilot:** 20+ campaigns, ≥1 channel partner who says
"I'll do this with my whole cohort next year," ≥2 real institution
disbursements completed end-to-end, K-factor measured (>1 ideally).

---

## 6. Vertical KPIs (on top of the global funnel)

- **Matriculation rate** of funded students (the killer outcome metric —
  this is what the channel and VCs buy).
- $ disbursed **directly to institutions** (trust proof; also the GMV).
- Campaigns **per channel partner** and channel-partner retention
  (do counselors come back next cycle?).
- Fraud / fund-diversion rate (should be ≈0 structurally — say so).
- Cost per acquired campaign (should trend to ≈$0 via the channel).

---

## 7. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Bursar/financial-aid offices won't accept third-party payments | Build a verified-payee directory; start with schools that accept third-party (most do via the student account #); standardize an intake for student ID + payment instructions |
| Student privacy (FERPA-adjacent via the school channel) | Counselor facilitates; student/guardian consents; Penyzen never needs protected records — only a payee instruction the family provides |
| Overfunding / student doesn't enroll / switches schools | Explicit restricted-fund + refund/reallocation policy (defined in the MLW build); communicate it up front on every campaign |
| Equity optics ("crowdfunding college is dystopian") | Lead with the matriculation-outcome and summer-melt framing; partner with mission-aligned nonprofits; never frivolous "party" framing |
| Seasonality | §4 mitigations: persistence funds + trade/CC + off-season channel build |
| Channel resistance ("one more tool") | Make the cohort dashboard genuinely useful and free; co-brand; minimize counselor effort (concierge in pilot) |

---

## 8. 30 / 60 / 90

- **30 days:** MLW shipped; 1 metro; ≥1 channel partner; 20+ campaigns;
  ≥2 institution disbursements done; funnel instrumented.
- **60 days:** matriculation outcomes starting; 2–3 channel partners;
  case studies drafted; refine the counselor kit from real feedback.
- **90 days:** a repeatable counselor-onboarding playbook; second metro
  or district expansion; deck slide = "X campaigns, $Y to institutions,
  Z% matriculation, K-factor, ~$0 CAC via the school channel."
